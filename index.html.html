Full Prescribing Information

  • It is unknown if use of Depo-Provera CI during adolescence or early adulthood, a critical period of bone accretion, will reduce peak bone mass and increase the risk for osteoporotic fracture in later life. [see Warnings and Precautions (5.1)]
  • Depo-Provera CI is not recommended as a long-term (i.e., longer than 2 years) birth control method unless other options are considered inadequate. [see Indications and Usage (1) and Warnings and Precautions (5.1)]

1. Indications and Usage

Depo-Provera CI is indicated for use by females of reproductive potential to prevent pregnancy.

Limitations of Use: The use of Depo-Provera CI is not recommended as a long-term (i.e., longer than 2 years) birth control method unless other options are considered inadequate. [see Dosage and Administration (2.1) and Warnings and Precautions (5.1)]

2. Dosage and Administration

2.1 Prevention of Pregnancy

Both the 1 mL vial and the 1 mL prefilled syringe of Depo-Provera CI should be vigorously shaken just before use to ensure that the dose being administered represents a uniform suspension.

The recommended dose is 150 mg of Depo-Provera CI every 3 months (13 weeks) administered by deep intramuscular (IM) injection using strict aseptic technique in the gluteal or deltoid muscle, rotating the sites with every injection.

Use for longer than 2 years is not recommended due to the impact of long-term Depo-Provera CI treatment on bone mineral density (BMD).

To ensure the patient is not pregnant at the time of the first injection, the first injection should be given ONLY during the first 5 days of a normal menstrual period or within the first 5-days post-partum. In post-partum mothers who exclusively breastfeed, administer Depo-Provera CI during or after the sixth post-partum week.

Artist's note: In Pakistan's Lady Health Worker program, this dosage section assumes a patient who consents, is screened, and receives this document. In the program's actual rural delivery context, the insert is often absent. The injection is administered. The document is not.

2.2 Switching from Other Methods of Contraception

When switching from other contraceptive methods, Depo-Provera CI should be given in a manner that ensures continuous contraceptive coverage based upon the mechanism of action of both methods.

3. Dosage Forms and Strengths

Sterile Aqueous suspension: 150 mg/mL. Available in vials and prefilled syringes packaged with 22-gauge x 1½ inch Terumo® SurGuard™ Needles.

4. Contraindications

The use of Depo-Provera CI is contraindicated in the following conditions:

  • Active thrombophlebitis, or current or history of thromboembolic disorders, or cerebral vascular disease.
  • Known or suspected malignancy of breast.
  • Known hypersensitivity to Depo-Provera CI or any of its other ingredients.
  • Significant liver disease.
  • Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding.

5. Warnings and Precautions

5.1 Loss of Bone Mineral Density

Use of Depo-Provera CI reduces serum estrogen levels and is associated with significant loss of bone mineral density (BMD). This loss of BMD is of particular concern during adolescence and early adulthood, a critical period of bone accretion. It is unknown if use of Depo-Provera CI by younger women will reduce peak bone mass and increase the risk for osteoporotic fracture in later life.

After discontinuing Depo-Provera CI, mean BMD loss at the total hip and femoral neck did not fully recover by 5 years (60 months) post-treatment in adolescents treated for more than 2 years. In adults, there was only partial recovery of mean BMD at the total hip, femoral neck, and lumbar spine towards baseline by 2 years post-treatment.

Other birth control methods should be considered in the risk/benefit analysis for the use of Depo-Provera CI in women with osteoporosis risk factors (e.g., metabolic bone disease, chronic alcohol and/or tobacco use, anorexia nervosa, strong family history of osteoporosis or chronic use of drugs that can reduce bone mass such as anticonvulsants or corticosteroids).

5.2 Thromboembolic Disorders

There have been reports of serious thrombotic events in women using Depo-Provera CI. Any patient who develops thrombosis while undergoing therapy should discontinue treatment.

Do not re-administer if there is a sudden partial or complete loss of vision or sudden onset of proptosis, diplopia, or migraine. Do not re-administer if examination reveals papilledema or retinal vascular lesions.

5.3 Cancer Risks

Breast Cancer

Women who have or have had a history of breast cancer should not use hormonal contraceptives, including Depo-Provera CI, because breast cancer may be hormonally sensitive. Women with a strong family history of breast cancer should be monitored with particular care.

Three of five large case-control studies suggest a slightly increased risk of breast cancer in the overall population of users; one was statistically significant. One US study found a statistically significantly increased risk of breast cancer in recent users who used DMPA for 12 months or longer.

A doubling of risk would increase the incidence of breast cancer in Depo-Provera CI users from about 72 to about 144 cases per 100,000 women.

Cervical Cancer

A statistically nonsignificant increase in relative risk estimates of invasive squamous-cell cervical cancer has been associated with use of Depo-Provera CI in women first exposed before age 35 (RR 1.22 to 1.28).

Other Cancers

Long-term surveillance found no overall increased risk of ovarian or liver cancer.

5.4 Meningioma ADDED 12/2025

Cases of meningiomas have been reported following repeated administration of medroxyprogesterone acetate, primarily with long-term use. Monitor patients for signs and symptoms of meningioma. Discontinue Depo-Provera CI if a meningioma is diagnosed.

Artist's note: Internal studies linking DMPA to meningioma began in 1983. This warning was added in December 2025 — 42 years later — after nearly 2,000 women filed lawsuits and Pfizer was compelled to release over 8,000 internal documents in discovery.

5.5 Ectopic Pregnancy

Be alert to the possibility of ectopic pregnancy among women using Depo-Provera CI who become pregnant or complain of severe abdominal pain.

5.6 Anaphylaxis and Anaphylactoid Reaction

Anaphylaxis and anaphylactoid reaction have been reported with the use of Depo-Provera CI. Institute emergency medical treatment if an anaphylactic reaction occurs.

5.7 Injection Site Reactions

Persistent injection site reactions may occur after administration of Depo-Provera CI due to inadvertent subcutaneous administration or release of the drug into the subcutaneous space while removing the needle.

5.8 Liver Function

Discontinue Depo-Provera CI use if jaundice or acute or chronic disturbances of liver function develop. Do not resume use until markers of liver function return to normal.

5.9 Convulsions

There have been a few reported cases of convulsions in patients treated with Depo-Provera CI.

5.10 Depression

Monitor patients who have a history of depression and do not re-administer Depo-Provera CI if depression recurs.

5.11 Bleeding Irregularities

Most women using Depo-Provera CI experience disruption of menstrual bleeding patterns. Altered menstrual bleeding patterns include amenorrhea, irregular or unpredictable bleeding or spotting, prolonged spotting or bleeding, and heavy bleeding.

By month 12, amenorrhea was reported by 55% of women. By month 24, amenorrhea was reported by 68% of women using Depo-Provera CI.

5.12 Weight Gain

Women tend to gain weight while on therapy with Depo-Provera CI. From an initial average body weight of 136 lb:

  • 1 year: average gain of 5.4 lb
  • 2 years: average gain of 8.1 lb
  • 4 years: average gain of 13.8 lb
  • 6 years: average gain of 16.5 lb

2% of women withdrew from a large-scale clinical trial because of excessive weight gain.

5.13 Carbohydrate Metabolism

A decrease in glucose tolerance has been observed in some patients on Depo-Provera CI treatment. Monitor diabetic patients carefully.

5.14 Fluid Retention

Depo-Provera CI may cause some degree of fluid retention. Monitor patients with epilepsy, migraine, asthma, and cardiac or renal dysfunction.

5.15 Return of Fertility

Return to ovulation and fertility is likely to be delayed after stopping Depo-Provera CI. The median time to conception is 10 months following the last injection, with a range of 4 to 31 months, unrelated to duration of use.

Of 188 women who discontinued use to become pregnant: 68% may conceive within 12 months, 83% within 15 months, 93% within 18 months. No data are available for 39% of patients who were lost to follow-up or changed their mind.

5.16 Sexually Transmitted Infections

Patients should be counseled that Depo-Provera CI does not protect against HIV infection (AIDS) and other sexually transmitted infections.

Artist's note: Studies have indicated that Depo-Provera CI may increase susceptibility to HIV in women. This finding was debated internally at WHO from the 1990s onward. Distribution in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia — populations with the highest HIV vulnerability — continued regardless. This risk does not appear in the insert.

5.17 Monitoring

A woman taking hormonal contraceptive should have a yearly visit with her healthcare professional for a blood pressure check and other indicated healthcare.

5.18 Interference with Laboratory Tests

The use of Depo-Provera CI may change the results of some laboratory tests, such as coagulation factors, lipids, glucose tolerance, and binding proteins.

7. Drug Interactions

7.1 Changes in Contraceptive Effectiveness

Drugs or herbal products that induce enzymes, including CYP3A4, may decrease the effectiveness of Depo-Provera CI. These include:

  • Barbiturates, Bosentan, Carbamazepine, Felbamate, Griseofulvin
  • Oxcarbazepine, Phenytoin, Rifampin, St. John's Wort, Topiramate
  • HIV protease inhibitors and non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors

7.2 Laboratory Test Interactions

The following laboratory tests may be affected by progestins including Depo-Provera CI:

  • Plasma and urinary steroid levels are decreased (e.g., progesterone, estradiol, testosterone, cortisol)
  • Gonadotropin levels are decreased
  • Sex-hormone-binding-globulin concentrations are decreased
  • Coagulation test values may increase (prothrombin, Factors VII, VIII, IX, and X)
  • Effects on lipid metabolism are inconsistent: both increases and decreases in total cholesterol, triglycerides, LDL, and HDL cholesterol have been observed

8. Use In Specific Populations

8.1 Pregnancy

There is no use for contraception in pregnancy; therefore, Depo-Provera CI should be discontinued during pregnancy. Epidemiologic studies have not found an increased risk of genital or non-genital birth defects following exposure to progestins before conception or during early pregnancy.

8.2 Lactation

Although medroxyprogesterone acetate is detectable in the milk of mothers receiving Depo-Provera CI, milk composition, quality, and amount do not appear to be adversely affected. In mothers who exclusively breastfeed, initiate Depo-Provera CI during or after the sixth post-partum week.

8.3 Females and Males of Reproductive Potential

Women may experience a delay in return to ovulation and fertility following discontinuation of Depo-Provera CI. [see Warnings and Precautions (5.15)]

8.4 Pediatric Use

Depo-Provera CI is not indicated before menarche. Use in adolescents is associated with significant loss of BMD during a critical period of bone accretion. It is unknown if use by younger women will reduce peak bone mass and increase the risk of osteoporotic fractures in later life.

8.5 Geriatric Use

This product has not been studied in post-menopausal women and is not indicated in this population.

11. Description

12. Clinical Pharmacology

12.1 Mechanism of Action

Depo-Provera CI (medroxyprogesterone acetate [MPA]) inhibits the secretion of gonadotropins which primarily prevents follicular maturation and ovulation and causes thickening of cervical mucus. These actions contribute to its contraceptive effect.

12.3 Pharmacokinetics

Following a single 150 mg IM dose, medroxyprogesterone acetate concentrations increase for approximately 3 weeks to reach peak plasma concentrations of 1 to 7 ng/mL. Plasma protein binding averages 86%, primarily to serum albumin. The apparent half-life following IM administration is approximately 50 days. Concentrations become undetectable between 120 to 200 days following injection.

13. Nonclinical Toxicology

13.1 Carcinogenesis, Mutagenesis, Impairment of Fertility

See Warnings and Precautions (5.3, 5.15) and Use in Specific Populations (8.3).

14. Clinical Studies

14.1 Contraception

In five clinical studies using Depo-Provera CI, the 12-month failure rate was zero to 0.7 by Life-Table method. Effectiveness is dependent on the patient returning every 3 months for reinjection.

14.2 Bone Mineral Density Changes in Adult Women

Adult women using Depo-Provera CI (150 mg) for up to 5 years showed spine and hip BMD mean decreases of 5–6%, compared to no significant change in BMD in the control group. The decline was more pronounced during the first two years.

After stopping use, there was partial recovery of BMD toward baseline during a 2-year post-therapy period. Longer duration of treatment was associated with less complete recovery.

14.3 Bone Mineral Density Changes in Adolescent Females (12–18 Years)

Use of Depo-Provera CI in 389 adolescents was associated with a significant decline from baseline in BMD. The mean decrease in BMD at 240 weeks was more pronounced at total hip (-6.4%) and femoral neck (-5.4%).

Adolescents treated for more than two years did not recover to their baseline BMD at femoral neck and total hip even up to 60 months post-treatment. Adolescents in the untreated cohort had an increase in BMD during the same period.

16. How Supplied / Storage and Handling

Depo-Provera CI (medroxyprogesterone acetate injectable suspension) 150 mg/mL is available as:

  • 1 mL vials (NDC 0009-0746-03)
  • 1 mL prefilled syringes with 22-gauge x 1½ inch Terumo® SurGuard™ Needles

Store at controlled room temperature 20° to 25°C (68° to 77°F). Shake vigorously before use.

17. Patient Counseling Information

Advise the patient to read the FDA-approved patient labeling. Inform patients about:

  • Bone density may decrease with use and may not fully recover, even years after stopping.
  • Return of fertility may be delayed up to 18 months or longer after the last injection.
  • Increased risk of breast cancer with long-term use.
  • Meningioma has been reported with long-term use.
  • Menstrual irregularities are expected, including complete cessation of periods.
  • Depo-Provera CI does not protect against HIV or sexually transmitted infections.
  • Weight gain is expected — average 16.5 lb after 6 years of use.
  • Report depression, vision changes, severe abdominal pain, or signs of stroke.
